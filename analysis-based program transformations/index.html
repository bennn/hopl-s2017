Materials on Analysis-Based Program Transformations

Mitch Wand, February, 2017

This page is for materials related to my lecture in CS 7480 History of Programming Languages, February, 2017

  • My slides, in ppt and pdf.
  • Example of old-fashioned flow equations (from Aho, Lam, Sethi, and Ullman, Compilers: Principles, Techniques, and Tools (2nd Edition), 2006).
  • Olin Shivers paper from 1991, showing the state of the art at the time. From Olin I learned the key idea of deriving control flow and data flow at the same time.
  • My notebook page from Spring 1991, showing what I was worrying about at the time. This is a page from a notepad at a DARPA meeting; my memory is that I wrote it at lunch to show to Paul Hudak.
  • My notes from what appears to be a pl-seminar presentation in January 1992.
  • Selective and Lightweight Closure Conversion, with Paul Steckler. This is our first paper on CFA and program transformation. This version is from POPL '94, the first of several versions of the paper; it eventually appeared in TOPLAS '97. There's a lot more stuff in this paper than I will discuss in class: we were trying to get towards polymorphic analysis and also to get "ordinary" and closure-converted procedures to interoperate.
  • Set Constraints for Destructive Array Update Optimization, with Will Clinger. This version is from the IEEE Conference on Computer Languages in 1998, a conference that existed only from 1988 to 1998. The journal version appeared in JFP in 1991, with detailed proofs. Christos Dimoulas obtained a more general result, including higher-order functions and arrays with non-scalar values, which appeared in VMCAI 2009

Here are a couple of papers that we are unlikely to get to in class, but which you may find of interest.

  • Constraint Systems for Useless Variable Elimination, with Igor Siveroni (POPL 1999), used the same techniques to transform a program by not passing values that were known dead. Again, this provided proofs for techniques that were discussed in Olin's dissertation.
  • CPS Transformation of Flow Information, with Jens Palsberg (JFP 2003), considered the question of how a CPS transformation changes the flow information of a program. This turned out to be a more subtle question than we realized, since it depends on both what you mean by a CPS translation and what you mean by 0CFA.
